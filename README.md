1. brand
brand is the main orchestration skill.

It does not only search for information. Instead, it coordinates the other skills and turns a brand investigation into a complete business workflow.

Best For
Researching whether a brand is worth representing
Evaluating a brand's market potential
Preparing for agency or distribution negotiations
Creating materials for management, negotiation, and sales teams
Workflow
Layer 1: Parallel Research
  ├─ Brand deep dive
  ├─ Competitor deep dive
  └─ Regional market research

Layer 2: Agency Evaluation
  └─ Use brand-agency-intel to build negotiation intelligence

Layer 3: Material Reuse
  └─ Use brand-3layer to generate audience-specific outputs
In short, brand works like a project manager for the full brand research and agency evaluation process.

2. brand-deep-dive
brand-deep-dive is used for public brand intelligence research.

It focuses on how a brand appears across public sources, search results, social platforms, consumer discussions, competitors, pricing signals, and historical development.

Example Triggers
Deep dive into [brand]
Research [brand]
Analyze this brand
Build a brand intelligence report
Main Output Dimensions
1. Brand awareness and visibility
2. Reputation and sentiment
3. Competitive landscape
4. Content ecosystem
5. User profile
6. Price perception
7. Timeline and development history
Purpose
This skill helps answer:

Does the brand have real visibility?
What do users actually think about it?
Are there obvious negative signals or risks?
Who are its main competitors?
Is the brand growing, stable, or declining?
How is the brand positioned in the market?
It is mainly used for intelligence gathering and market perception analysis.

3. brand-agency-intel
brand-agency-intel is used to evaluate whether a brand is suitable for agency, distribution, or market representation.

Compared with brand-deep-dive, this skill is more business-oriented. It focuses on whether the brand is worth representing, how to negotiate, what risks exist, and what leverage can be used.

Example Triggers
Agency negotiation
Brand agency evaluation
Distribution opportunity
Agency scoring
Negotiation report
Brand representation analysis
Recommended Inputs
Brand name
Product category
Brand origin region
Target agency region
Cooperation goal
Example:

Evaluate whether [brand] is suitable for China market representation. The category is skincare, the brand is from Korea, and I want to assess whether we should pursue exclusive distribution.
Core Outputs

Product strength

4. brand-3layer
brand-3layer is actually a four-audience reuse framework.

It does not continue researching the brand. Instead, it transforms existing research and agency intelligence into practical materials for different audiences.

Example Triggers
Brand talking points
Three-layer reuse
Four-layer reuse
Management briefing
Sales script
AI sales assistant
Brand-side preparation
Internal alignment
Four Output Layers
1. Negotiation Layer
   For the user or BD team. Extracts negotiation leverage, pressure points, concession space, and negotiation scripts.

2. Brand-Side Management Layer
   For the brand owner's management team. Helps them understand the market opportunity, reduce hesitation, and align expectations.

3. Internal Management Layer
   For the user's own company. Summarizes project value, risk, investment expectations, operating assumptions, and decision logic.

4. Sales / Terminal Layer
   For sales teams, stores, livestream hosts, shopping assistants, and AI sales agents. Converts brand research into consumer-facing sales language.
Risky Language Control
The skill avoids risky or inappropriate wording such as:

original factory goods
tail goods
counterfeit
copycat
poor quality control
you cannot sell this product
Instead, it reframes sensitive issues into business-safe language such as:

low consumer awareness
market education opportunity
channel communication gap
positioning needs to be rebuilt
Recommended Usage Flow
A complete workflow looks like this:

Step 1:
Use brand-deep-dive to research the brand.

Step 2:
Use brand-agency-intel to evaluate agency value and negotiation strategy.

Step 3:
Use brand-3layer to create negotiation, brand-side, internal management, and sales materials.

Step 4:
Use brand to orchestrate the full process when a complete end-to-end workflow is needed.
Example Prompts
Use the full workflow:

Use the brand workflow to research [brand], evaluate whether it is suitable for China market representation, and generate materials for negotiation, brand-side management, internal management, and sales teams.
Use an individual skill:
